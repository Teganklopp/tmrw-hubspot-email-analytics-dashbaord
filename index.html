02
Channel anatomy

Where the volume actually comes from.

You sometimes describe your sending as "workflows more than campaigns" — the data backs that up. Marketing (workflows + broadcasts) does 87% of the work. The remaining 13% is the 1-to-1 channel — a mixed bag we'll unpack below.

What "Marketing" means here

Anything sent through HubSpot's Marketing Email tool

Workflows, automated lifecycle sequences, broadcasts, the Weekly Science Email, GOV-1, From the Lab, Order Confirmations, CEO welcomes — all of these are marketing emails in HubSpot's data model, even though they feel like different things to you. They're tracked via per-contact lifetime counters (hs_email_delivered, etc.) automatically maintained by HubSpot.

What "1-to-1" means here

Single emails logged on individual contacts

These are outbound EMAIL engagement records — emails written to one contact at a time, either composed inside HubSpot or sent from a rep's Gmail/Outlook and auto-logged. The 325 number is a mix: real sales rep messages, plus some HubSpot system notifications (like inbox-connected confirmations). The pure sales-rep portion is likely 200–280.

Strategic takeaways

What this data is telling you to do.

Six recommendations, ranked by how much they'd move the needle on lifecycle retention and revenue. These are derived directly from patterns in your send data.

01
Audit the 600-contact pile-up at "2 emails received".
74% of your email recipients have received exactly 2 emails. That's the size of your welcome flow exit point — and an enormous opportunity. Are these contacts meant to stop there (e.g., they're leads in a slow nurture)? Or did a workflow stall out before completing? Pull a sample of 20, check what workflow they entered and where it ended, and look for a missing step. Adding even one well-timed follow-up here could 2× total send volume.
Action: pull 20 random "2 email" contacts from HubSpot → trace their workflow history
02
Build a re-engagement workflow for the 64 "drifting" contacts.
These are people who've received 3 or more sends since their last open or click. Standard playbook: a single break-up email ("Still want to hear from us?") with a clear opt-in to keep receiving emails. The ones who re-engage get back into the main flow. The ones who don't get suppressed — which protects your deliverability and inflates your already-strong 55% open rate further.
Action: create a HubSpot list filter on hs_email_sends_since_last_engagement ≥ 3
03
Map what happens after the welcome ends.
Weekly Science Email pulls double duty as both your recurring welcome email for new sign-ups and your ongoing newsletter — which is elegant, but it means the transition point matters enormously. Do new sign-ups smoothly roll into the next edition the following week? Or does the welcome version of Edition 1 get a different follow-up path than the recurring stream? Audit the workflow exit conditions: when does a contact stop being "new" and start being "ongoing", and which content do they get at that handover. This is where lifecycle programs leak.
Action: trace one sample contact's path from sign-up through their first 4 weekly sends
04
Watch the GOV-1 blast's downstream effect this week.
"Clinical Transparency" went to a large slice of your active base today, and it's a non-routine broadcast. Watch the open rate on the next few sends from your regular workflows — clinical/compliance emails are dense and can either deepen trust (and lift engagement) or burn list freshness. If next week's Weekly Science open rate dips below 50%, that's the cost of the blast. If it stays at 55%+, you've earned permission to do more transparency comms.
Action: snapshot today's open rate baseline, recheck in 7 days
05
Your 1-to-1 channel is mostly reactive. Use it as a lifecycle hook.
The raw outbound:inbound ratio is 1:1.08, but the outbound count includes HubSpot system messages. Strip those out and your sales reps are likely sending around 200–280 real 1-to-1 emails against 350+ inbound replies — meaningfully reactive. That's healthy coverage but the proactive lane is mostly empty. Highest-leverage move: pair lifecycle stages with 1-to-1 outreach. A personal note from a rep 24h after [OB-4] Welcome Call Invitation would lift call bookings without scaling the team. Workflow → human handoff is where automated lifecycle programs become real relationships.
Action: add a sales rep notification trigger to your OB-4 workflow
06
Suppress or label your test contacts.
"Dharma SM" with 72 emails (100% open rate), "Cara Lab", "Ben Onboarding", "Alice New", "Ethan Retest", "Diana Active", "Fiona Blocked" — these read like internal QA contacts cycling through every workflow. They're inflating your sent volume by hundreds and slightly skewing your open rate up. Either tag them with a test_contact = true property and filter them out of dashboards, or graduate them into a separate test portal.
Action: create a HubSpot list of likely test contacts → review → suppress from analytics

Methodology & gaps

How this report was built. And what's missing.

Every number in this briefing came directly from your HubSpot, queried live via the MCP integration on 22 May 2026, 3:55 pm AEST. The data pipeline:

Marketing totals. Computed by summing HubSpot's per-contact lifetime counters — hs_email_delivered, hs_email_open, hs_email_click, hs_email_bounce — across all 810 contacts who've received any marketing email. These counters track every marketing email sent via HubSpot's email tool — your workflows, automated sequences, broadcasts, and one-off sends. Transactional API sends and SMS are not in these counters.
1-to-1 totals. Pulled from the EMAIL engagement object in HubSpot CRM, filtered to hs_email_direction = EMAIL (outbound). These are any single-recipient emails logged on contact records — a mix of real sales rep messages and some HubSpot system notifications (e.g., "inbox connected" confirmations). The pure sales-rep portion is probably 200–280 of the 325. Inbound replies (353) are counted separately and don't include system messages, so the real reactive ratio is closer to 1:1.3 than 1:1.08.
Workflow catalog. Built by inspecting the distinct values of hs_email_last_email_name across the 200 most recently sent contacts. This gives a reliable view of which workflows are currently firing, but it doesn't catalogue every email that has ever existed in your account.
Engagement cohorts. Built by filtering on hs_email_last_open_date (active = opened in last 7 days), hs_email_sends_since_last_engagement (drifting ≥ 3), and hs_email_optout.

What's not in this report. The CRM-side MCP integration doesn't expose per-workflow analytics, per-email-asset send counts, or the workflow trigger/exit logic itself. To get those, you'd need direct API access to HubSpot's Marketing Email API and Workflows API. If you want a deeper dive — workflow-by-workflow performance, sequence completion rates, attribution to revenue — that's the next data layer to unlock.

The data we have says: you're doing better than industry average, the foundation is healthy, and the biggest lifts are operational, not strategic.
